SNORKELING

Snorkeling is an activity that involves swimming or floating on the water's surface while wearing a snorkel mask and a breathing tube, known as a snorkel, to allow the individual to observe and explore the underwater world. It typically involves observing marine life and coral formations in shallow waters, allowing the snorkeler to breathe through the snorkel tube while their face is submerged. Snorkeling is often done in clear water environments such as oceans, seas, lakes, or lagoons, and can be enjoyed by people of various skill levels.

DIVING

Scuba diving is a form of underwater diving where the diver uses a self-contained underwater breathing apparatus (scuba) to breathe, allowing for greater freedom and mobility underwater. This typically involves the use of a compressed air tank and regulators, mask, fins, and a dive suit. Scuba diving can be carried out for recreational, professional, or scientific purposes and offers the opportunity to explore marine life, underwater landscapes, and sunken structures.
